Part I — The Artist
Dan Katz, Illustrator
Everything here was assembled by AI from your public portfolio, Reedsy profile, and web presence — to show what's possible when machine intelligence meets artistic depth.
Dan Katz is an Atlanta-based oil painter and illustrator whose work lives at the intersection of painterly realism and narrative imagination. Trained at Columbus College of Art & Design (2005–2009) and sharpened through nearly a decade of Illustration Master Class attendance (2009–2018) and Smart Art School advanced studies, Dan brings a rare combination: classical oil technique applied to speculative and fantastical subjects.
His work doesn't look like AI-generated illustration. That's the point. In an era flooding with synthetic imagery, Dan's paintings carry the texture of physical media, the warmth of impasto brushwork, and the intentionality of traditional composition. Every piece is a decision tree of color, light, and narrative — something algorithms approximate but don't replicate.
Based in Atlanta, freelancing since 2022 after 7+ years as a graphic designer at Kason Industries. Currently open for commissions and contracts through Reedsy and direct outreach.

What Makes Dan's Work Dan's
"Painterly realism and thoughtful design that blends into intriguing and emotional works"
Translation: Dan doesn't chase photorealism. He leaves the brush visible. Every stroke is a conscious decision — not rendered, authored. The "thoughtful design" means compositions are structured with graphic intent, not just observed. This is illustration in the truest sense: images that communicate, not just depict.
"He has always appreciated both the serious and the fun, and looks to balance the thought-provoking and the entertaining"
Translation: The dragon-commuting-to-work piece exists in the same portfolio as Monte Cristo. This is rare. Most illustrators pick a lane. Dan's range means he can match any author's tone — from grimdark to whimsical — without forcing the work. That's a commercial superpower for book illustration.
"Oil paintings celebrated for their richness, conveying subtle emotions with a watercolor-like softness"
Translation: Dan's oil handling is unusual — thin in places, creating translucency typically associated with watercolor. This creates atmospheric depth that digital painting struggles to match. It's a technical signature — his paintings breathe in a way that AI-generated imagery fundamentally cannot.
"Experienced in developing book-based illustrations and can communicate and work with clients"
Translation: Not just a painter — a collaborator. Dan understands that book illustration is interpretive work. He reads the manuscript, talks to the author, and makes visual decisions that serve the story. This client-facing skill is where AI struggles most — the nuanced conversation between artist and author about what a scene means.

Part II — Working Together
Where AI Fits Your Workflow
Not selling you AI. Showing you where it removes friction from work you're already doing — so more of your hours go to the canvas.

The honest premise

AI can generate images. You know this. You've probably seen it flood your market with mediocre fantasy art. Here's what AI cannot do:

It can't load a brush with cadmium yellow and drag it through titanium white with the pressure that comes from painting the same stroke ten thousand times. It can't read a manuscript and feel which moment is the cover. It can't sit with a client and hear the difference between what they say they want and what they actually need.

Your hands, your eye, your judgment — those are the product. AI is useful for everything around that: finding clients, managing content, analyzing markets, handling the business of being an artist so you can focus on the art of being an artist.

One Painting, Thirty Posts

You finish a piece. AI generates a full month of social content: detail crops with commentary, palette analysis graphics, composition overlay posts, "how I built this" narrative threads, before/after studies. Captioned. Hashtagged. Scheduled.
Your input: One high-res scan + 2 sentences about the piece.
